Skill. Reskill. Upskill.

Skill refers to the abilities, knowledge, and expertise that an individual possesses in a particular area, whether it is a technical skill such as coding or a soft skill such as communication. In today's competitive job market, having a diverse set of skills can help individuals stand out and adapt to changing workplace demands.
 
Reskilling refers to the process of learning new skills to transition into a new career or to keep up with changes in the job market. This may involve learning completely new skills or building on existing ones to improve employability. For example, someone who previously worked in retail may need to reskill to work in the healthcare sector due to increased demand for healthcare workers.
 
Upskilling, on the other hand, refers to the process of enhancing or improving existing skills to stay relevant in the workforce. This may involve taking courses, attending workshops, or obtaining new certifications to keep up with the latest trends and technologies in a particular field.
